SMART WALKS

About the project

Developed by researchers and graduate students at the UCL Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis (CASA), Smart Walks is a prototype web application for location-based augmented reality walking tours. This prototype is being tested as part of an MSc dissertation on the value of such an app for postgraduate students.

What does participation involve?

Using the Smart Walks application, you will complete a short, 10-15 minute walk about networked urban technology, titled "Technology in the City". In addition, you will complete one short survey before the walk, which will ask about past experiences with walking tours; and another survey after the walk, which will ask for feedback on the Smart Walks application. Both surveys are completely anonymous.

The trial should take no longer than 30 minutes in total.

Safety disclaimer

Please remain aware of your surroundings while using the Smart Walks application.
